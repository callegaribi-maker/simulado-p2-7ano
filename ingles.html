Grammar: Comparisons – (not) as … as / enough

▼

📐 (not) as + adjective + as

as + adjective + as → igual/tão ... quanto
not as + adjective + as → menos ... do que
Examples

➤ My house is as big as yours. (tão grande quanto)
➤ This sofa is not as comfortable as the armchair. (menos confortável)
➤ She is as tall as her brother.

📐 (not) + adjective + enough

adjective + enough → suficientemente ... (para)
not + adjective + enough → não é suficientemente ...
Examples

➤ The room is big enough for a bed. (grande o suficiente)
➤ This chair isn't comfortable enough to sit on.
➤ She isn't old enough to drive.

⚠️ Atenção à ordem! "Enough" vem DEPOIS do adjetivo: ✅ big enough    ❌ enough big
📋

Grammar: Have to / Don't have to (Obrigações)

▼
FormaSentidoExemplo
have toobrigação (necessidade)I have to do my homework.
don't / doesn't have tonão é obrigado (sem necessidade)She doesn't have to cook.
Do you have to...?pergunta sobre obrigaçãoDo you have to work today?
More Examples

➤ Students have to wear uniforms. (obrigação)
➤ You don't have to come early. (não é necessário)
➤ He has to clean his room every day.

💡 have to vs. must: Ambos indicam obrigação, mas have to é mais comum no dia a dia e must é mais formal/interno.

▼

🔀

Grammar: Zero & First Conditionals (Condicionais)

▼

🌡️ Zero Conditional – Fatos e verdades gerais

If + Simple Present, Simple Present

Usa-se para fatos científicos, verdades universais, situações sempre verdadeiras.

Examples

➤ If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.
➤ If it rains, the ground gets wet.
➤ If you cut yourself, you bleed.

🎯 First Conditional – Situações reais/possíveis no futuro

If + Simple Present, will + verb

Usa-se para situações possíveis no futuro com consequências reais.

Examples

➤ If you fall, you will hurt yourself.
➤ If she doesn't study, she will fail the test.
➤ If it rains tomorrow, we won't go to the park.

🔑 Dica: A ordem das orações pode ser invertida! "You will hurt yourself if you fall." (sem vírgula quando o "if" vem depois)

Reading Comprehension Tips

▼

🎯 Estratégias para a prova

1. Scanning: Leia as questões ANTES do texto. Saiba o que procurar.

2. Skimming: Leia o texto rapidamente para pegar a ideia geral.

3. Context clues: Use o contexto para adivinhar palavras desconhecidas.

4. True/False/Not Given: O que não está no texto = Not Given.

5. Linhas importantes: As respostas geralmente estão em ordem no texto.

🧠

Quiz – Test Yourself!

▼

🎯 Answer the questions:

1. "If you touch fire, you _____ burned." Which tense is this?

✅ Zero conditional! Fact/truth: If + present, present → "get" (gets)

2. What is "cotovelo" in English?

✅ Elbow = cotovelo! Wrist = pulso, Ankle = tornozelo, Shoulder = ombro.

3. "This room isn't _____ enough for a wardrobe." Fill in the blank:

✅ "big enough" – adjective comes BEFORE "enough"!

4. "You _____ run in the hospital." (proibição)

✅ Mustn't = prohibition! Don't have to = not necessary (different meaning!)

5. What is "lavar a louça" in English?

✅ Do the dishes = lavar a louça! Do the laundry = lavar roupa.
